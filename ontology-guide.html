The map, explained

One card per ontology element: what it is, whether the rebuild may touch it, and how to say it well. Running example: an OpCo accounts-payable workflow. — keep this handy when a team asks "what goes in this box?"

Farrier-side reference — uses honest vocabulary (teardown, receiving team). Never project this.
LOCKED — survives the teardown, server-enforced HOW — yours to change in the rebuild REBUILD — new build material
The litmus test for anything locked: could this sentence appear, unchanged, in two completely different designs? If yes, it's problem-space — lockable. If it names a mechanism, artifact, threshold, or step, it's HOW in disguise — and locking it smuggles the old design past the teardown.

Trigger

LOCKED
invoice arrives

The event in the world that sets the workflow off. Not the first thing your team does — the thing that happens to you. The redesign still has to answer the same event, which is why it's locked.

  • "an invoice arrives" · "stock dips below safety level" · "a candidate applies"
  • "AP clerk checks the inbox each morning" — that's your first step (HOW). The trigger is whatever put something in the inbox.

Inputs

LOCKED as factsform is HOW
inputs: supplier master data · PO history · delivery confirmations

What the world supplies that the work consumes. Captured as facts, never formats — the fact survives the teardown, the container doesn't.

  • "we know what we ordered (PO history)" · "supplier bank details exist, verified annually"
  • "the AP Excel tracker" — that's the current container. The fact is "we track what's been paid"; the spreadsheet is HOW.

Persona

accountable / served LOCKEDoperators are HOW
OpCo GMaccountable — owns the outcome
suppliersserved — feel the outcome
AP clerkoperator — does today's steps

People in the workflow, in three flavours. Accountable (signs, answers for it) and served (benefits) are locked — the rebuild must keep serving the same people. Operators are part of today's HOW: the rebuild may re-imagine who (or what) does the steps, but every operator must land somewhere — stays / transforms / removed-justified-by-the-design. "Freed up for higher-value work" is rejected as a landing.

  • "the GM is accountable — she signs the payment run"
  • "the GM approves every invoice" — that's an operator role + a rule. Lock the accountability, not the approval step.

A phase is a chapter of the work; moments are where time goes and things turn, nested inside. Both are pure HOW — they don't survive the teardown. A moment flagged painful ⚠ becomes an area of concern: the receiving team inherits the problem ("reconciliation regularly eats days"), never the steps. Put numbers on pain — quantified pain gives the brief its teeth.

  • "manual reconcile — 3 days at month-end, two people" (pain with a number)
  • "reconciling is annoying" — no volume, no cost; too thin for a stranger to act on.

Intent

LOCKED
decide which payments to release, and whento protect the credit line

The WHY — the decision the workflow drives. The most important block on the map and the most commonly faked. Two traps: the artifact ("produce the board pack" — a report isn't a reason) and the restatement — ask why a process exists and the default answer is the process restated ("why monthly reporting? to report monthly"). The push: "what happens because of this?" then "what would you do differently if it said something else?" No answer to the second = there is no decision — that's a finding, not a capture failure.

  • "decide which payments to release and when, to protect the credit line"
  • "decide how much to brew and where to ship it, before the lead time decides for us"
  • "the GM needs to see cash flow" — "seeing" is a dashboard in disguise. What does she decide with it?
  • "produce the monthly board pack" — an artifact, not a reason.

Outcome

LOCKED
suppliers paid on time; credit terms intact

The change in the world when the workflow has done its job. Persona-free, mechanism-free — a state of the world, not a description of who does what. (And outcome ≠ output: an output is an artifact, which is HOW.)

  • "suppliers paid on time; credit terms intact; cash position never surprises anyone"
  • "GM monitors the dashboard and approves anything over £100" — a persona + a mechanism + a threshold. Three pieces of HOW wearing the outcome's hat.

AI-native agent

REBUILD only
match & flag agentin: invoice+PO · decides: match? · out: queued payment · fails to: GM

The rebuild's new build material — an agent doing real work, not a chatbot bolted on (the rabbit rule). An agent block must name input · decision · output · failure-path, and must draw its escalation: an ⚡ with no arrow to any human block fails the autonomy audit. Unicorn ≠ headless — somebody still answers for it.

  • "matches invoices to POs; mismatch >2% → flags the GM with its working shown"
  • "AI handles invoicing" — no decision, no failure path, nothing to audit. Over-simplification.

Kept constraint

REBUILDarrives as a candidate
two-person approval on payment runskept — fiduciary requirement
"GM approves everything >£100"candidate — pressure-test me

Constraints arrive at the rebuild as candidates, not verdicts — each wearing "candidate — pressure-test me." The team and Coach interrogate every one: genuinely binding (legal, audit, physical) → kept 🔒; habit dressed as policy → killed ✂️, onto the constraint ledger. The ledger (held vs killed) is the star of the share-out.

  • "two-person approval — fiduciary requirement, confirmed → kept"
  • "GM approves >£100 — 'policy'" — turned out to be a 2019 habit after a one-off fraud scare → ✂️ killed, and the ledger says why.

Orphan

parked — blocks the gate
"finance sometimes rings the supplier directly to sort it out"

A true thing that fits nowhere yet. It is never deleted — it's parked visibly in the tray and the Coach chases it ("where does this live?"). Orphans block the readiness gate: a map with homeless truths isn't ready for a newcomer. Most orphans turn out to be the interesting part — undocumented exception paths.

  • parked, chased, then placed — usually revealing a hidden exception path or a missing moment
  • quietly dropped because it didn't fit — if it's true and homeless, the map is wrong, not the fact.

Arrows

drawn by the team

Solid = flow. Dashed = conditional, labelled with the condition. In the rebuild, one arrow is load-bearing: ⚡ agent → 👤 human is the drawn escalation path — its absence is what the autonomy audit flags, and it's checkable with no AI at all.
