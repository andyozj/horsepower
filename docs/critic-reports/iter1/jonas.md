## Problems

1. **[BLOCKER] Stranded reveal overlay sat over Rebuild and blocked the collapsed Coach button — the exact bug CLAUDE.md claims is fixed.** `11-rebuild-coach-collapsed.png` is not the Rebuild canvas at all — it's the **full-screen navy "REDESIGN / DON'T RETROFIT" stamp scrim** (`#reveal.on`, `role="alertdialog"`). The driver's `rebuild-canvas` selector matched in the DOM underneath, but every click on the collapsed Coach face (`.railtoggle.coachbtn` / `[data-testid=rail-toggle]`) was intercepted: `run.out` logs 30s of `<div id="reveal"> intercepts pointer events` retries before the driver threw (`run.out:56–366`). CLAUDE.md's QA-pass notes explicitly say "stranded reveal overlay auto-dismisses on phase change … it used to sit over Share … and blocked their reckoning clicks." Here it sat over **Rebuild** and blocked the Coach. A member who never personally clicked "Let's build" is locked out of the Coach for the whole Rebuild phase. Severity: **critical** — the Coach is the product, and this hides it behind an un-dismissable scrim. Evidence: `11-rebuild-coach-collapsed.png`, `run.out:46–366`.
   - **Honesty caveat (driver-owned, partial):** the Farrier fired the swap *later* than the driver's 240s reveal-wait budget, so the driver's `#reveal.on` wait timed out and it **skipped the explicit `#reveal-go` ("Let's build →") dismiss** that a normal participant presses. BUT the spec's promise is **auto-dismiss on *phase change***, and the canvas had already mounted underneath while the scrim was still modal and click-eating — so the auto-dismiss-on-phase-change guarantee did not hold, and any participant who tabs away during the staged +1.5s CTA, or whose CTA never renders, hits exactly this lock-out.

2. **[HIGH] Verbatim-identical Coach reply across an unrelated long dump, a short question, AND a chip — the repeat the question bank is supposed to prevent.** The 253-char invoice brain-dump, the short question "is our intent a real decision?", and the "Newcomer check" chip ALL returned the byte-identical string: *"That term might drown a newcomer — spell it out. Could someone who just joined run with this?"* (`observations.log:30,32,35`; visible in `05-braindump-result.png`, `06-short-question.png`). CLAUDE.md claims "question bank never repeats the same line back-to-back (server)" — that guard only blocks *consecutive* repeats; it does nothing for relevance, so three different inputs in a row collapse onto one canned line. For a long dump *and* a short intent question, that reply is also **off-topic** (the user asked about intent/decisions; the Coach lectured about jargon). Reads as canned-and-dumb, not canned-but-good. Severity: high — directly undercuts "the Coach is a character who reads along." Evidence: `observations.log:30,32,35`, `05-braindump-result.png`, `06-short-question.png`.

3. **[MEDIUM] Chip-label mismatch: the spec/driver expected "What's thin?" but the rendered chip is "What's thin?" with a curly apostrophe AND the panel also surfaces a different label set.** The visible chips are "What's thin?", "Run the Newcomer check", "Push me on WHY" (`observations.log:24`), but the driver's `text=What's thin` (straight apostrophe) **missed** (`observations.log:34`, `[chip missing]`). ## What's genuinely good

- **The Coach face is genuinely consistent across every surface — this is the strongest thing here.** Same red-cap-+-ring avatar (`coachAvatar()`) appears in: the lobby "Meet your Coach" intro panel (`01-lobby-full.png`), the post-saddle "here's a warm-up" panel (`03-after-saddle.png`), the Surface rail header "the Coach" (`04-surface-coach-open.png`), each red coach bubble, and the round collapsed-rail button (`09-rail-collapsed.png`). It reads as one character, not a chat box. The lobby "Where to find me" promise row ("look in the right-hand rail of every map — tap my face if I'm tucked away") is a real, thoughtful onboarding touch.
- **The long-dump handling is honest and safe — the toast is exactly right.** After the 253-char dump in offline mode: my words were preserved verbatim in a "Jonas"-attributed user bubble (`05-braindump-result.png`, crop), and an amber toast said *"Coach is offline — long dumps won't auto-structure. Your words are safe in the thread; draw the blocks or park lines in the parking lot."* (`observations.log:29`). That is the designed degradation done well — no silent data loss, no fake AI, a concrete next action. The rail header also carries a persistent honest status line *"Coach is offline — the map & checks still work"* (`05` crop). This is how to degrade with dignity.
- **"Push me on WHY" produced a genuinely good, on-methodology reply:** *"Who actually owns this step — a named role, not 'the team'? And why them?"* (`observations.log:36`, `07-chips-replies.png`). That is precisely the constraint-raw-material / accountable-persona push the methodology wants. When the canned line fits, it fits well.
- **Live collaboration + author-dots work and are visible.** On Jonas's screen the shared map carried Iris's 7 blocks (`observations.log:39–41`); teammate-authored nodes carry a small colored author-dot (teal dot on "tickets resolved before customer notice", `08` crop), and **my own** "handover at shift change" block does NOT carry a teammate dot — correct per presence §13 (teammate-authored only). The map feels live and shared, not solo.
- **My block landed and immediately invited its WHY.** Dropping a Moment opened the inspector "the back of the card → why does this moment matter?" (`08` crop) — the WHY raw-material loop is wired into authoring, not bolted on.
- **Rail collapse is clean and the Coach stays present.** Collapsing the rail (`09-rail-collapsed.png`) hands the full-bleed map to the user while leaving the round red Coach face + "reading along…" peek pill above the zoom control — discoverable without shouting.

## Verdict

**When you can reach it, the Coach is a character, not a chat widget** — consistent face across lobby/rail/bubbles/collapsed-button, honest about being offline, a warm onboarding promise, and at least one chip ("Push me on WHY") that lands the methodology cleanly. The offline degradation is handled with real care (verbatim-safe dump + honest toast + persistent status line). **But two things keep this from passing:** (1) a **stranded reveal overlay blocked the Rebuild Coach entirely** for a member who didn't click through the reveal — a regression of a bug CLAUDE.md says is fixed, and it hides the product's centerpiece; and (2) the question-bank's **relevance** is thin — three unrelated prompts collapsing onto one verbatim line reads canned-and-dumb. Fix the overlay (blocker) and add input-keyword routing + a wider non-consecutive de-dup window to the offline bank, and the character holds. **Rebuild "fair skeptic" role-shift: NOT assessable this run** — no Rebuild-Coach evidence was captured (driver threw; supp re-join landed in the picker).
