WHY IS THIS HAPPENING?

It’s not the employees; it’s the bottlenecks above them.

Employees
  • Already using AI — and leading the change
  • 3× more ready than leaders assume
  • They reject useless change, not change itself
Leaders
  • Choose the safe, bounded bet
  • Redesign = risk, commitment, politics
  • The real brake on transformation

So, we don’t pitch harder…
We strive to become a trusted advisor, not just a tech builder.

Sources: McKinsey & Harvard Business Review

THE THREE T’S

The three things blocking us are the three things we give back

Trust
Leaders have to trust us. In return, we give them confidence in the redesigned process. We do that by demonstrating our understanding of their processes and how intents are addressed.
Time
They have to give us their time to show us how work really happens. In return, we prove how our recommendations will give time back to their business.
Turf
To leaders, redesign may mean that roles getting redrawn. To address this, we hand back a business process that is cleaner and simpler for their teams work and a clear path for retraining their people to drive the new car.
RETROFIT VS. REDESIGN

How do we spot the difference?

Retrofit
  • Find a step where AI can help.
  • “How can I improve this?”
  • Process can live without the solution.
Redesign
  • Question if the workflow should exist.
  • “How would I design this today?”
  • AI-native: it collapses without AI.

CLOSING

Wrapping things up

1the why
A faster horse is still a horse.
Bolting AI onto an existing process makes it marginally faster, but it doesn't transform anything. Real value comes from redesigning the process around what AI makes possible, not decorating the one you already have.
2the how
Trust is the bottleneck, not the tech.
The reason redesign doesn't happen isn't capability; it's that leaders won't bet on it until they trust us. We earn that through the Three T’s — Trust, Time, and Turf.
3the what
We redesign ourselves before anyone lets us redesign them.
If we can't do it to ourselves, no one will dare let us do it for them. The next move is real: find one workflow, name its owner, and start redesigning.
