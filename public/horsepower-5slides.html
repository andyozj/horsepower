…and the WHY behind every one — that’s the gold.
Goal: map it so a newcomer could run it.

The Coach watches for…

  • A person folded into a step — every named person becomes their own persona, never a faceless “sign-off”.
  • The served party left implicit — who is this ultimately for?
  • An intent that’s an artifact (“a report”) instead of a real decision.
  • A missing WHY on a load-bearing step or role.
  • “That’s just how it’s done” — habit dressed as a constraint.
stage 2 · Rebuild

Tear it down. Rebuild it AI-native.

The original how is stripped away. The team gets an abstract brief — the need, the areas of concern, candidate constraints, and the people inventory — and rebuilds the workflow with Agent blocks doing the real work.

The Coach watches for…

  • Retrofit — AI bolted onto the old steps, the work unchanged in shape.
  • “Freed up for higher-value work” hand-waves — every person must land: stays, transforms, or removed-and-justified.
  • A “rule” that’s really habit — is the constraint real, or just assumed?
  • Convergence back to the original — process clichés creeping in.
  • A vague keep — it makes the team state the claim out loud so the debrief can judge it.
the through-line

The Coach provokes. It never decides.

It has no domain truth — it can’t tell you the right answer. Its whole job is to turn a silent fake-keep into a stated claim the team owns, and to make retrofit visible while there’s still time to fix it.

Pushes on the WHY

Make the team feel heard, then push. It challenges experts — it doesn’t catch them out.

Builds as you talk

Voice-first or typed — it interviews you and draws the map live, person by person.

Provokes, never rules

It surfaces the question; the humans make every call. The debrief — not the AI — is the judge.

Never stalls the room

Every gate is rule-based and works offline. If the AI is down, the workshop still runs.
